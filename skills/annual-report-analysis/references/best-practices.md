# Best Practices, Examples, and Quality Guidelines

This document provides examples, tips for success, common mistakes to avoid, and the comprehensive quality checklist for **annual report analysis**.

## Example Headlines

### Good Annual Report Analysis Headlines:
- "Nike FY2024: Margin Recovery Offsets Soft Top-Line Growth - Thesis Improving"
- "Tesla FY2024: Auto Margin Pressure Persists, Energy and FSD Optionality Remain Key"
- "LVMH FY2024: Fashion & Leather Holds Up, Wines Still Weak - Long-Term Thesis Intact"
- "Apple FY2024: Services and Buybacks Support Quality, China iPhone Trends Bear Watching"

### Bad Headlines (Avoid):
- "Annual Report Analysis" (too generic, no takeaway)
- "Company Filed 10-K" (states the obvious, no analysis)
- "FY2024 Results Review" (no company name, no view)
- "Annual Update" (too vague, no investment implication)

## Tips for Success

1. **Lead with the conclusion**: Start with the most important annual takeaway, not a data dump.

2. **Focus on what changed this year**: Emphasize what is new in the current fiscal year rather than repeating generic company background.

3. **Quantify everything**: "Improved profitability" means little; "operating margin expanded 180bps to 17.4%" is useful.

4. **Explain the drivers**: Do not just report that revenue grew or EPS declined. Explain why.

5. **Prioritize financial quality**: Annual analysis should pay close attention to margins, cash flow, balance sheet strength, and capital allocation.

6. **Use multi-year context**: Annual reports are best understood in context. Show 3-year or 5-year trends where relevant.

7. **Separate fact from outlook**: Clearly distinguish reported annual results from management guidance or forward-looking commentary.

8. **Use primary sources first**: Annual earnings release, 10-K / 20-F / annual report, transcript, investor presentation, then secondary sources only if needed.

9. **Be selective, not encyclopedic**: This is not an initiation report. Focus on the most decision-relevant issues.

10. **Let charts do useful work**: Annual trend charts, segment mix charts, and cash flow / leverage charts are usually more valuable than dense tables.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too quarterly in style**: Do not write an annual report analysis like a 24-hour post-earnings quick take.

❌ **Too comprehensive**: Do not turn this into an initiation-length report.

❌ **Too descriptive**: Do not just summarize the 10-K. Add interpretation.

❌ **Ignoring cash flow**: Annual analysis must examine operating cash flow, free cash flow, and capital allocation.

❌ **Ignoring balance sheet quality**: Debt, liquidity, leverage, and working capital matter.

❌ **Overusing generic background**: Do not spend pages explaining what the company does unless directly relevant.

❌ **No thesis implication**: Results must connect to whether the long-term investment thesis is improving, intact, or deteriorating.

❌ **Vague language**: Avoid phrases like "solid performance" or "mixed year" without numbers and explanation.

❌ **No source discipline**: Every important fact, figure, and table must have a source.

❌ **Plain text URLs**: All links in the final DOCX should be formatted as clickable hyperlinks, not raw URLs.

❌ **Fabricating expectations**: If consensus or estimates are unavailable, say so and fall back to guidance, prior-year comparison, and multi-year trend analysis.

## Comprehensive Quality Checklist

Run through ALL items below before delivering the report.

### Content and Analysis

**Annual Performance:**
- [ ] Report leads with the main annual conclusion
- [ ] Revenue, EPS / net income, and margin performance are quantified
- [ ] Year-over-year changes are clearly shown
- [ ] Major drivers of growth or decline are explained
- [ ] Comparison vs expectations, guidance, or prior year is included where possible

**Business Performance:**
- [ ] Segment analysis is included where relevant
- [ ] Geographic analysis is included where relevant
- [ ] Product line or operating unit breakdown is included where relevant
- [ ] Major strategic changes or business mix shifts are identified

**Profitability and Financial Quality:**
- [ ] Gross margin, operating margin, and net margin are analyzed where relevant
- [ ] Operating cash flow is discussed
- [ ] Free cash flow is discussed
- [ ] Balance sheet strength is evaluated
- [ ] Debt, liquidity, leverage, or working capital are addressed where relevant
- [ ] Capital allocation is discussed where relevant (buybacks, dividends, M&A, capex)

**Outlook and Thesis:**
- [ ] Management outlook for the next year is discussed if available
- [ ] If no guidance is provided, this is explicitly noted
- [ ] Thesis impact is clearly stated
- [ ] Risks are identified
- [ ] Catalysts are identified where relevant
- [ ] Estimate implications are discussed where relevant
- [ ] Valuation implications are discussed where relevant

### Format and Length

- [ ] Report is 8-12 pages unless user requested otherwise
- [ ] Page 1 functions as an executive summary
- [ ] Title is specific and conclusion-driven
- [ ] Report structure matches annual report analysis, not quarterly earnings update
- [ ] Appendix is used only if needed

**Tables:**
- [ ] 1-3 summary tables included, not comprehensive statement dumps
- [ ] Tables have clear column headers
- [ ] Tables are readable and decision-useful
- [ ] All tables have source lines at the bottom

**Charts:**
- [ ] 8-12 charts embedded throughout the document
- [ ] All charts have "Figure X - [Title]" caption above
- [ ] All charts have "Source: [Source]" line below
- [ ] Charts focus on annual trends and major changes
- [ ] Charts use professional styling
- [ ] Charts match the text discussion

### Citations and Sources

**Figure and Table Citations:**
- [ ] Every figure has a specific source with document name and date
- [ ] Every table has a specific source with document reference
- [ ] Source citations include page numbers or slide numbers where applicable

**Reported Results Citations:**
- [ ] Annual results are cited to the annual earnings release and/or 10-K / 20-F / annual report
- [ ] Segment and geographic data are cited to the filing or investor presentation
- [ ] Cash flow and balance sheet data are cited to the filing

**Expectation and Outlook Citations:**
- [ ] Expectations / consensus references cite the source and date where available
- [ ] Guidance or outlook is cited to the annual release, transcript, or investor presentation
- [ ] Prior-year or prior guidance references are cited when discussing changes over time

**Statistics and Commentary:**
- [ ] Key statistics have footnotes with sources where needed
- [ ] Management quotes cite speaker name and source document
- [ ] Forward-looking statements are clearly identified as such

**Hyperlinks:**
- [ ] All URLs in the final document are clickable hyperlinks, not plain text
- [ ] Hyperlinks use meaningful display text
- [ ] Hyperlinks are formatted correctly in the Word document
- [ ] SEC filings are hyperlinked to EDGAR or official filing pages
- [ ] Company IR materials are hyperlinked where available
- [ ] No raw URLs are displayed in the body unless specifically requested

**Sources Section:**
- [ ] "Sources & References" section is included at the end of the report
- [ ] Section lists all major annual materials with dates
- [ ] All listed materials have clickable hyperlinks where available
- [ ] Estimate / expectation sources are listed if used
- [ ] Prior-period references are listed if used in comparisons

### Accuracy

- [ ] Numbers match the company's reported results exactly
- [ ] Calculations are correct
- [ ] Growth rates are correct
- [ ] Margin changes and basis-point calculations are correct
- [ ] Valuation math is correct if included
- [ ] Charts match the tables and narrative
- [ ] Company name and ticker symbol are correct
- [ ] Fiscal year references are correct throughout
- [ ] Filing dates are correct
- [ ] Management guidance or outlook is described accurately
- [ ] No stale or outdated materials are used by mistake

### Timeliness

- [ ] Verified all data is from the requested or latest fiscal year
- [ ] Did not rely on memory alone; actively searched for current annual materials
- [ ] Annual earnings release date is correct
- [ ] 10-K / 20-F / annual report filing date is correct
- [ ] Supporting materials (transcript, webcast, presentation) match the annual reporting period
- [ ] No prior-year filing is mistakenly treated as current

### Writing Style

- [ ] Lead with conclusions, not background
- [ ] Use numbers early and often
- [ ] Be direct and concise throughout
- [ ] Focus on what changed this year
- [ ] Avoid vague language
- [ ] Institutional tone maintained
- [ ] Terminology consistent throughout
- [ ] No informal language
- [ ] Proper financial notation used consistently

## 5-Minute Final Review

Quick final pass before sending the report:

1. **Page 1**: Is the overall conclusion clear and useful?
2. **Numbers**: Do key figures match the annual release / filing exactly?
3. **Sources**: Spot-check 3-4 figures or tables - do they all have proper sources?
4. **Thesis**: Is it clear whether the long-term thesis improved, stayed intact, or weakened?
5. **Cash Flow / Balance Sheet**: Did the report cover financial quality, not just revenue and EPS?
6. **Charts**: Are all charts embedded, labeled, and relevant?
7. **Length**: Is it within the requested or default range?
8. **Hyperlinks**: Are the main source links formatted correctly in the DOCX?
9. **Fiscal Year**: Is the year correct throughout the document?

If all items check out, the report is ready for delivery.
